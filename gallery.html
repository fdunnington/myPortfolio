Case Study

Client: Criminal Justice Hub
Industry: Non-Profit / Legal
The story so far
To overhaul a complex legal information site, we used Object-Oriented UX to bring clarity and structure to a large, tangled content set. Starting with the objects users interact with — not just the actions they take — gave us a clearer framework for design, making the site more intuitive, scalable, and user-focused.
GitHubDrupalGDSOOUXFigma
What shaped the work

User research and analytics showed that people were struggling to find information because content was disconnected and difficult to navigate. Rather than starting with navigation patterns, we focused on the core content types users actually needed. Defining six key objects and their relationships helped bring order to the system and gave the team a shared, scalable framework to build on.

Seriously effective
The modular structure made content easier to find, easier to manage, and easier to grow — with fewer mistakes and better collaboration across the team.

Case Study

Client: FlexiTog
Industry: E-Commerce
The story so far

A European workwear brand was expanding from B2B into direct-to-consumer sales, but users were struggling with the website. Rather than rushing into a major redesign, we took a closer look at the content structure — and found that a few well-targeted changes could make a big difference.

FigmaHotjarGA4
What shaped the work

Initial concerns pointed to the navigation, but analysis revealed deeper issues with how content was structured and presented. A detailed audit and user behaviour data shaped our approach. We reorganised the information architecture, refined user journeys, and adapted the mobile layout — all designed to make it easier for users to browse, search and buy.

Seriously effective

A 214% increase in purchase events, 12% more revenue, and fewer clicks to get to the cart — all from focused, strategic content improvements.

Case Study

Client: Inghams Walking
Industry: Travel
The story so far

A leading active travel company needed content that matched the quality of their holidays. Outdated copy was losing them sales and overwhelming their support team. By rewriting for clarity, trust, and search, we helped boost bookings and reduce customer confusion.

UX WritingSEOCRO
What shaped the work

User confusion was driving up support queries and stalling bookings. The fix wasn’t flashy—it was about clarity. We focused on writing that was vivid but practical, tightened internal linking, aligned language with SEO and accessibility best practice, and made the offering easier to understand for a wider audience.

Seriously effective

Clearer content reduced friction, increased bookings and improved customer trust—while cutting down repetitive queries to the support team.
