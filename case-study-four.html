From static site to supportive hub: Migrating from WordPress to Drupal

Navigating the Criminal Justice System can be daunting, especially for those unfamiliar with it. A non-profit organisation dedicated to providing clear and accessible information needed a major website overhaul. The existing WordPress site was no longer sufficient for the organisation’s evolving needs and lacked the functionality required to deliver a top-notch user experience.

The task was to migrate the site from WordPress to localgov Drupal to better meet their mission and serve their users, and to perform a content overhaul along the way.

The challenge: Unlocking the potential

The non-profit’s website was packed with valuable information, but finding it was a bit of a challenge due to:

  • Disorganised content: Although the information was present, the layout was confusing. A lack of internal linking resulted in siloed content that was hard to navigate.
  • A lack of clarity and accessibility: Complex terminology and jargon made it difficult for users unfamiliar with the justice system to understand.
  • Outdated information: Keeping legal resources current is crucial. There was an opportunity to streamline the content and ensure it reflected the latest developments.

The Fix: A collaborative redesign

The goal was to transform the Criminal Justice Hub website into a user-friendly and informative resource. Here’s how we approached it:

  • Content audit: We conducted a comprehensive audit of the existing content, identifying areas for improvement and opportunities to leverage the website's full potential.
  • Building a strong framework: We crafted a well-structured Information Architecture (IA), providing a clear framework for organising and presenting information effectively.
  • Clarity first: Rewriting content in plain, concise language was essential, ensuring users could easily understand their rights and knew where to turn for help.
  • A familiar face:  We used the Government Design System (GDS) to create a user-friendly and familiar-feeling portal similar to other trusted government organisation sites.

The outcome: Empowering users through information accessibility

The site is now on track to becoming the comprehensive resource it was intended to be:

  • Improved navigation: A user-friendly structure helps visitors find the information they need with ease.
  • Clearer communication: Content is now clear and accessible, equipping users with the knowledge needed to navigate the complexities of the justice system.
  • A foundation for growth: The robust IA provides a strong base for future development, allowing the website to adapt and expand to meet evolving user needs.

This project showcases how a strategic redesign and content overhaul can transform a crucial public resource, making it more intuitive and impactful for users seeking guidance through the complex systems and processes
