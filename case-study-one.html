From frustration to summer bliss: Re-vamping a travel leader's web and print content

Can well-written and well-structured content be the key to unlocking a wider customer base and boosting sales? For this client, a well-established leader in the package holiday industry, the answer was a resounding yes. Here's the story of how we transformed their re-branded summer product website and brochure, tackling outdated content, navigation woes, and a need to attract new customers without alienating their loyal base.

The challenge: A website stuck in times gone past

Imagine a travel brochure filled with faded photos and forgotten destinations. That's what the client's website had become. Inaccurate content, a confusing layout, and a lack of focus on their exciting summer product – European lake and mountain holidays – meant frustrated users and missed opportunities.

The fix: Fresh content, clear navigation, and SEO savvy

We knew a complete overhaul was needed. Here's how we tackled the challenges:

  • Content that captivates: We replaced tired information with engaging descriptions tailored to each destination. Think vivid descriptions of crystal-clear lakes and invigorating mountain hikes.
  • An engaging funnel: We implemented clear internal linking and a user-friendly layout, making it a breeze for visitors to find the information they wanted.
  • Accessibility for all: Ensuring the website met WCAG3 accessibility standards was crucial.
  • SEO smarts: Keyword optimisation put the focus firmly on walking, attracting a broader audience searching for active but accessible mountain adventures.
  • Speaking the right language: We developed a consistent tone, style, and terminology guide that reflected the brand's re-invention while retaining its core identity.

The outcome: Sales success is the sweet reward

The end result is a website that speaks volumes – not just to their existing loyal customer base, but to a whole new generation of summer explorers.

The re-brand was a triumph. The company saw bookings from a wider customer base, proving you can attract new audiences without neglecting your existing fans. Navigation became a breeze, leading to fewer frustrated users and a significant increase in conversion rates. More accurate information allowed users to make better-informed choices, and the content worked harder to sell an exceptional product.
