From static site to supportive hub: Rebuilding the Criminal Justice Hub website

The Criminal Justice System is a complex system to navigate and is unfamiliar territory for many. The Criminal Justice Hub, a vital online resource for people in England and Wales, strives to provide clear and accessible information to those in need of information.

Their original website, built in WordPress, needed an update to fully meet the goals of the service and the needs of its users.

The challenge: Unlocking the potential

The Criminal Justice Hub's website contains a wealth of valuable information. However, navigating the site and finding information was challenging due to:

  • Disorganizsed content: While the information was present, its structure could be improved to enhance findability.
  • A lack of clarity and accessibility: Complex terminology and jargon could make it difficult for users unfamiliar with the justice system to understand.
  • Outdated information: Keeping legal resources current is crucial. There was an opportunity to streamline the content and ensure it reflected the latest developments.

The Fix: A collaborative redesign

The goal was to transform the Criminal Justice Hub website into a user-friendly and informative resource. Here's what we did:

  • Content audit: We conducted a thorough review of existing content, identifying areas for improvement and opportunities to leverage the website's full potential.
  • Clarity first: Rewriting content in clear and concise language was a priority, ensuring users could easily understand their rights and navigate the legal system.
  • A familiar face:  We migrated the website from Wordpress to Drupal, utilizing GDS (Government Design Standards) to create a user-friendly experience that mirrored trusted government websites like gov.uk.
  • Building a strong structure: A well-structured Information Architecture (IA) was developed, providing a clear framework for organizing and presenting information.

The outcome: Empowering users through information accessibility

The Criminal Justice Hub website is now well on its way to becoming the comprehensive resource it was envisioned to be:

  • Improved navigation: A user-friendly structure makes it easier for visitors to find the information they need, reducing confusion and frustration.
  • Clearer communication: Content is presented in a clear and accessible manner, empowering users with the knowledge they need to navigate the complexities of the justice system.
  • A foundation for growth: The solid IA provides a strong base for future development, allowing the website to adapt and expand to meet evolving user needs.

This case study is a testament to the power of strategic website design in creating a valuable resource for the public. By prioritizing clarity and user experience, the Criminal Justice Hub website is moving towards becoming the comprehensive resource it was built to be, meeting user needs, and providing a valuable service for anyone navigating the Criminal Justie System.
