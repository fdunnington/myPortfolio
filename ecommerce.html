Modernising a WooCommerce Site

A project to re-energise a ten-year-old publishing website built with WooCommerce/Wordpress, with a focus on improving user flow and site performance.

Work With Me

The Problem

The team at Piccadilly were looking to upgrade and modernise a tired site. Key goals were increasing site speed and allowing users to easily browse their product range.

Their existing site was built in Wordpress using WooCommerce for its ecommerce platform The goals were to modernise the site, offering users a fresh new design that was aimed at attracting a younger market. The company were keen to keep the site on the existing platform as the staff were familiar with how Wordpress/WooCommerce work, and the company wanted updates/changes to be possible to do internally.


The Process

User personas: Developed detailed dossiers on three different archetypal target users as reference points to guide us in our journey to solve our client’s ultimate problem.

Feature prioritization: With data from our extensive research, we prioritized a list of possible features — some essential, others less so — that we would need to factor into our solution.

Information architecture: We re-evaluated the current website, and based on our research, reorganized the main navigation, footer and contact flow

User flows: Guided by our personas, we wrote stories for how our users would navigate through the new site.

Visual design: We began with crude early sketches, progressed to low-fidelity wireframes, and finally to a working medium-fidelity prototype of mobile and desktop versions of the website. We would test and validate our designs every step of the way.

User testing: With a working prototype, we tested our design on users, recording and documenting their verbal and nonverbal feedback.

Iterative design: From our testing, we made extensive changes to our design to deliver a final high-fidelity mock-up to the client and dev-team
