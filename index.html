UI Design & Prototyping

Purpose: Working out how it looks and functions
What UI design and prototyping involves:

Creating visuals of pages and (where necessary) developing these into interactive user flows to determine exactly how all the content is related

Wireframes can range from very basic hand-drawn sketches to low-fidelity mockups, depending on the complexity of whatâ€™s being constructed and how defined ideas are.

When and why it's important:

Deciding on the UI is an essential step in the process of developing a site or product. Like anything, how it looks and functions is a public reflection of what it is. It affects how it's preceived by users and is an opportunity to see what's working and what's not before developing. It's also an opportunity to test colours and typography for web accessiblity.

How deep the process needs to be depends a little on each project - the more complex, the more advisable it is to invest more time at this stage to avoid costly development changes and delays. Measure twice, cut once. The more detailed you go at this stage, the faster the build process will be.

Wireframes

Purpose: Structural clarity
What wireframes are:

Wireframes add flesh to the bones. If the information architecture is the blueprint then the wireframes are the architectural drawings.

Wireframes can range from very basic hand-drawn sketches to low-fidelity mockups, depending on the complexity of whatâ€™s being constructed and how defined ideas are.

When it might help to build wireframes:

The purpose is to convey ideas effectively without spending excessive time/money on high-fidelity mock-ups.

Wireframing is also the first stage of looking at where different information will sit on different screen sizes, making sure that everything necessary is included but without overcrowding.

Information Architecture

Purpose: The product blueprint
What Information Archtecture is all about:

What goes where, why, and how it links to everything else.

Information Architecture is the blueprint of a digital product. It sets out how content is structured, categorised, and connected so that users can navigate with ease. It can take the form of site maps, hierarchies, or flow diagrams, depending on the complexity of the project and how much detail is needed at that stage.

When it might help you to invest in IA:

Information Architecture is useful at the start of a project, when you need to clarify how content should be structured before design or development begins.

It also helps when reorganising an existing site or app, especially if content has grown unevenly over time and navigation no longer feels clear or intuitive.

Design Systems

Purpose: The product blueprint
What a design system is:

A design system is the single source of truth for a productâ€™s visual language and interaction patterns. It defines how elements look, behave, and work together.

It can include everything from colour palettes and typography to component libraries and usage guidelines, with the level of detail shaped by the needs of the organisation.

Why you might need a design system:

The purpose is to bring consistency and efficiency to design and development by creating a shared library of components, patterns, and guidelines.

A design system helps teams work faster and more cohesively, reducing duplication and ensuring a seamless user experience across products and platforms.
